Mar 2026
Why I switched from Anthropic to Gemini mid-build
Credits ran out at the worst moment — mid-feature, mid-demo. Migrated the Bitez AI scanner to Gemini in 48 hours. What I learned: your infrastructure choices should survive a crisis. Also: gemini-2.5-flash is genuinely good for structured extraction.
Bitez
Feb 2026
The auth bug that took three days to find
JWT tokens were valid. The middleware said 401. Turned out Zustand wasn't rehydrating before the route guard ran. The fix was one line. The lesson was about assuming your state management works. It doesn't always. Trust the logs.
Bitez
Jan 2026
What I got wrong about training a sarcasm detector
First attempt was sentence-level. Accuracy looked fine. Real-world performance was poor — sarcasm is relational, not syntactic. Retrained with conversation history as context. That's when it started working. Context is everything. This is what Ghost is really about.
Ghost
Dec 2025
Building real-time routing when the internet is unreliable
Socket.io works great in demos. In Kashmir, connectivity drops mid-delivery. Built a reconnection layer with local state persistence. Riders now sync when connection restores. The infrastructure you build for constraint is always better than the infrastructure you build for ideal conditions.
Bitez
Nov 2025
Why Ghost is written in C++ and not Python
Python is where you experiment. C++ is where you commit. Ghost is a commitment. I want to understand inference at the weight level — memory layout, cache behaviour, what actually happens when you run a transformer. You can't see that from PyTorch. You have to go lower.
Ghost
Oct 2025
The saffron farmer problem — why Kashmir Export exists
A farmer in Pampore grows the most expensive spice in the world. He sells it for a fraction of the global price because he has no direct access to buyers. Three middlemen take the margin. Kashmir Export is the attempt to remove them. First prototype is live. One farmer, one product, one direct sale. Proof of concept.
